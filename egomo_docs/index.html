Attachment of FT sensor, gripper and base board to the UR5.

Note: With the standard attachment direction of FT sensor and gripper to the flansh, the spiral power cable has to be brought to the opposite side of the FT sensor to correctly attach the cable to the Egomo sensor head. In order to prevent the cable to become unnecessarily long, it is threaded through the side holes of the FT sensor.
Note: With this Xamla Egomo sensor head attachment the UR5 arm can move relatively freely. Especially wrist 3 can rotate completely free.

Alternative 1: Preparation of the Logitech camera for front connection with the base board.

Alternative 2: Rotated attachment of FT sensor and gripper to the UR5.

If the force torque (FT) sensor and gripper are attached to the UR5 in a 180 degree rotated way, you do not have to run the spiral cable through the FT sensor, to get it on the opposite side. This has the advantage, that the spiral cable might be equipped with a plug at its end, so that it can simply be plugged into the Egomo casing. Thus, unscrewing of the Egomo casing and screwing of the spiral cable into a plug at the IO board will not be necessary any more. However, a disadvantage of the rotated attachment is that the axis labels printed on the FT sensor are no longer valid and that some files like the unified robot description format (URDF) might have to be adapted to the rotated attachment.
